## Lesson 2: Limiting Beliefs & Mindset Breakthroughs

## Limiting Beliefs That Hold Asian Creators Back (and How to Reframe Them)

Before we jump into creating your offer and making money, we need to address something way more important: **your mindset**.

I’ve seen it over and over (and dealt with it myself when I first started)—sometimes the biggest thing standing in your way isn’t strategy… it’s the stories you tell yourself.

So in this lesson, I’m breaking down some of the most common limiting beliefs I hear from new coaches/creators, and how to flip them so they stop holding you back.


---

### **Why This Matters**

If you’re carrying around these beliefs, it doesn’t matter how good your offer is—you’ll self-sabotage before you even give yourself a fair shot. When you reframe them, you open the door for all the strategies you’re about to learn to actually work.


---

### **Common Limiting Beliefs & Reframes**

**1. “I’m not experienced enough.”**
If you got yourself from point A to point B, you already have a blueprint someone else needs. That’s credibility. Plus—you’ve got this community of coaches to lean on for support and growth.

**2. “No one will pay me that much.”**
You’re offering a shortcut to solve someone’s long-standing problem. People pay for speed, clarity, and results—that’s exactly what you bring to the table.

**3. “I don’t want to be salesy.”**
Selling = serving. If you know your program can help, it’s your responsibility to offer it to those who need it.

**4. “What if I fail and look stupid?”**
The right people will support you. The wrong people’s opinions don’t pay your bills—so they don’t get a vote.

**5. “The market is too saturated.”**
No one else has your story, your style, or your way of coaching. Your uniqueness is your competitive edge, and that can’t be duplicated.

**6. “English isn’t my first language, what if I’m not polished enough on camera?”**
Perfection isn’t what connects people—authenticity is. Your accent, culture, and story make you relatable and memorable.

**7. “My parents/family won’t approve.”**
It’s normal to feel pressure to choose the “safe path.” But your path doesn’t have to look like theirs. When you succeed, you show them what’s possible—and their support often follows.


---

### **Action Step**

1. Grab a piece of paper.
2. Write down your **top 3 limiting beliefs** about yourself or your business.
3. Next to each, write the **empowering reframe**.
4. Read them out loud every morning this week.
5. Remember that you’re in good hands here. This community is built to help you win.


---

### **Closing / Next Step**

When you shift your mindset, the strategies in this program will work 10x better—because you’ll finally give yourself permission to succeed.

## Lesson 3: Offer Creation

## Structuring Your Offer for Recurring Revenue

One of the most important parts of building a stable online coaching business is creating offers that **build recurring revenue over time**.

If you only sell short-term packages—like a $1,000 four-month program—you’ll constantly have to refill spots every few months. That makes it harder to scale.

Instead, I like to have **both** a short-term and a long-term option, but I always **incentivize the long-term** so it builds up my recurring monthly income and gives me stability.


---

### **Example Pricing Structure**

**4-Month Program - **$250/month (10% discount if paid in full → $900 total)

**12-Month Program - **$200/month (10% discount if paid in full → $2,400 × 0.9 = $2,160 total)

### **Example Deliverables (Standard Online Fitness Coaching)**

1. Custom meal plan
2. Custom training program
3. Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins
4. 24/7 communication


---

### **Why This Works**

- The **monthly price** of the long-term option looks cheaper, so more people lean toward it.
- Incentivize clients to commit longer so it builds your monthly recurring revenue.
- Fewer “revolving door” cycles of replacing clients every few months.
- You can even make the 12-month program slightly more appealing by adding **bonus deliverables**, like:
    - Supplement guide
  - Lifetime price lock
  - Extra coaching call each quarter
  These small additions make the long-term option feel like a better value without dramatically increasing your workload.


---

### **Creating Your Pricing Sheet**

A well-designed pricing sheet is powerful because it **guides your prospect’s eyes** toward the option you want them to choose.

**Layout Example:**

- Left column: 4-Month Program ($250/month)
- Right column: 12-Month Program ($200/month) — highlighted as “Most Popular” or “Best Value”
- Deliverables listed below, with extra bonuses in the 12-month column

The visual setup makes the long-term plan more attractive at a glance.

## Lesson 5: Contracts

## Using Contracts in Your Coaching Business

### Why Contracts Matter

1. **Professionalism**
    - You’re receiving payment and delivering a service—treat it as a professional exchange.
  - A signed contract shows clients your program is legitimate and worth taking seriously.
2. **Accountability**
    - Clients commit to their payments and responsibilities.
  - You commit to delivering the agreed-upon service.
  - Signing makes both parties more likely to honor the agreement.
3. **Stability & Security**
    - Reduces drop-offs and “ghosting.”
  - Gives you predictable recurring revenue.
  - If a client wants to leave early, you can refer to the signed agreement to encourage completion.

> **Note:** The goal isn’t necessarily to take legal action—it’s to set clear expectations and maintain commitment.



---

### Core Elements of a Good Contract

1. **Payment Terms** – Amount, frequency, and method of payment.
2. **Duration** – Length of the program (e.g., 4 months or 12 months).
3. **Client Responsibilities** – What the client must do (attendance, communication, following plans).
4. **Coach Responsibilities** – What you will deliver (programs, check-ins, support).
5. **Confidentiality** – Protecting sensitive client information.
6. **Disclaimers & Release** – Legal protections, health disclaimers, and liability release.


---

### Example Use Case

If a client tries to drop out halfway through:

- Remind them they signed for the full term.
- Highlight their wins and progress so far.
- Reinforce that you’ve upheld your side of the agreement.


---

3. **Use it consistently** with every new client before starting work.

## Lesson 7: Sales Scripts

# Lesson: How to Take a Sales Call from Beginning to End

In this lesson, I’ll walk you through exactly how to take a sales call from start to finish. Sales can feel intimidating especially for us Asians who don’t want to come off as “salesy.” This lesson will teach you a sales script that’s authentic and not pushy. It combines being yourself while unlearning fears around selling a good service.


---

## Why Sales Skills Matter

Sales skills directly impact your success as a coach.
Closing 40% of calls instead of 20% doubles your revenue, even assuming equal effort. So improving your sales approach is one of the most effective ways to grow your business.


---

## 3 Rules for High-Ticket Sales Calls

1. **Get on video:** Use Zoom, FaceTime, or any video platform. Face-to-face interaction builds trust.
2. **Take notes:** Good salespeople always take notes during calls.
3. **Be confident:** Be authentic and confident.

*How to build confidence?* Be prepared.


---

## Pre-Meeting Preparation

Before each call, do these tasks to feel ready and confident:

- Send a video reminder the morning of the call.
Example: “Hi John, just wanted to send a quick video to make sure we’re all set for our call today. Super excited to chat! It should take around 30 minutes. Bring a pen and paper—you’re going to get a ton of value. Talk soon!”
- Review the lead’s info and notes from Calendly or your CRM.
- Record client name, date/time of meeting, phone, email, attendance (Y/N).
- Do anything that loosens you up: jump around, play music, meditate, yell, whatever helps you get confident


---

## Sales Call Structure (30–40 minutes)

1. **Build connection (1 min)**
2. **Frame the call (1–2 min)**
3. **Client intro / discovery (5 min)**
4. **Follow-up questions (5–10 min)**
    - Clarify goals
  - Why now?
  - What’s worked or not?
  - Create the gap
  - Bridge the gap
5. **Program demo/explanation (5 min)**
6. **Close (5 min)**
7. **Handle objections (5 min)**


---

### Step 1: Build Connection

Start casual and friendly. Example:
“Where are you calling from?”


---

### Step 2: Frame the Call

Explain the call’s purpose. Example:
“This call is to get to know you, see if we’re a good fit, and if we're a good fit, I'll invite you to join the program and all I ask is that you give me a hell yes or a hell no. Sound good?”


---

### Step 3: Client Introduction

Ask the client to briefly introduce themselves and what motivated them to fill out the interest form.

"So I'll hand you the mic. Give me a brief introduction of yourself and what got you on this call with me today?"


---

### Step 4: Follow-Up Questions (Discovery)

- Ask permission to collect personal details like age, weight, height.
- **Clarify goals:** “If you worked with me for 12 months, what does success look like to you?" AND/OR "What is your #1 fitness goal before the end of the year?"
- **Why now?** "Curious… why did you decide to reach out NOW? Like why haven’t you gotten a coach like 6 months ago, or even 6 months from now?
- **Fitness history: **past sports, past attempts, what worked/didn’t?
- **Create gap:** "Now that I know what you want, why do you think you haven’t been able to accomplish these goals on your own?" AND/OR "Where do you feel like a fitness coach would fill in the gaps for you?"
- **Bridge gap:** GIVE VALUE HERE. “So John thank you so much for sharing all this with me, I really appreciate your honesty throughout this call. I think I have everything I need. It sounds like to me that you need [insert their needs] to help you fix [insert pain points] and the best course of action for you is to [insert value]. How does that sound? Perfect because that’s literally what my program helps people do…let me go ahead and share my screen to show you what it looks like inside the system, cool?”


---

### Step 5: Program Demo / Explanation (3-5 min)

- Showcase system training plan (1 min)
- Showcase meal plan (1 min)
- Showcase best client result (with their permission ofc) (1 min)


---

### Step 6: Closing

- Preface the price sheet
    - “So John I told you in the beginning of the call I’d let you know if I could help you and the good news is you are definitely the type of person I can get results for and I can already tell just from this call you’re going to crush it in my program…would you like to hear about how I can help?”
- Show price sheet
    - “So I have two different options. There’s a short term 4-month program and a 12-month sustainability program. TBH I think for you and your goals the 12-month program makes the most sense. The 4-month program is $250 / month and the 12-month program is $200 / month. Each program has a discount where if you pay in full on this call I’ll give you a 10% discount for either program. Which program are you leaning towards?”
- NOW SHUT YOUR MOUTH AND LET THEM SPEAK!


---

### Step 7: Handling Common Objections (5 min)

- I need some time to think about it
    - **Sell the Exit: "**Of course bro, thats no worry at all. When do you think you can get me an answer by? Okay, so can I reach back out this time on Friday then? Awesome bro thank you I will reach out to you then. As I schedule that call, mind if I ask..."
  - **Clarify Objection: "**...just to be super clear for our chat on Friday, what exactly between now and then do you need to think about?"
- It’s just quite expensive
    - **Move from Finances to Program:** "Of course bro totally understand. I guess one quick question, finances aside, is this program what you were looking for?"
  - **Price vs Value: **"Gotcha, so it’s just finances? Mind if I ask you one question. If you were to envision yourself exactly where you wanted to be by the end of your program, would the investment be worth it?"
  - **Sell with Confidence:** "I know exactly what I need to do with you to get you there, and I KNOW if you follow protocol I can get you to where you want to be. And to be honest, my first step in helping you is this call right here, to commit and allow me to help you. If you think the investment will be worth it, I promise you it will be worth it because I will get you there.
- Need to talk to my spouse
    - **Relate: "**I totally get that. Does your spouse know we’re speaking today?"
  - **Play Scenarios: "**What would happen if they say no? What would happen if they say yes?"
  - **Isolate Decision: "**I mean it sounds like you already know what they’re going to say. At the end of the day, this is a decision for you. You’re going to be the one showing up, going to the gym, doing your cardio, cooking your meal prep. This is your decision. So I get you want to talk it out with someone but you can talk it out with me, the person that’s going to be helping you for the next 12 months. What do you want for yourself?"


---

### Step 8: Cement the Sale (5 min)

- Collect Payment
    - **Send the Stripe Payment Link or Trainerize Product Link: **“Awesome man let me send over the link to get set up and I’ll walk you through it to make sure I can get you through all the next steps.”
- Cement the Sale
    - **Ease Negative Emotions and Handle Cold Feet: **"Awesome, I see it on my end. Congrats! You just made the best decision of your life! How are you feeling right now?...That’s completely normal. I’m so excited for you, just know I am 100% on your team, I will not let you down, and you just made the best decision of your life."
- Next Steps
    - **Give Tasks and Set Expectations: "**So what’s going to happen next is I’ll send you an email walking you through your next steps. If you can get that done today, I will program for you starting tonight. It typically takes me a couple days to complete a program, but I’ll have it done ASAP and I’ll reach out to schedule your onboarding call once it’s complete, and we can hop on an onboarding call as soon as Monday. Sound good?Awesome get those next steps done and we’re good to go. Super excited to work together and we’ll chat very soon."

# MODULE 2: CONTENT & TRUST


## The Trust Recession: How to Build Trust Online

## Lesson Summary: The Trust Recession & How to Build Trust in the Online Economy

We are currently in what I call a **Trust Recession**.

In today’s online economy, *everyone* is selling something. Coaches, creators, mentors, gurus — every scroll leads to a CTA. Because of this, audiences now approach content with **automatic skepticism**. Trust is no longer assumed; it must be **earned**.

This lesson breaks down *why* trust is at an all-time low and gives you a **practical framework** to rebuild it — without begging for clients, over-selling, or trying to look like a guru.


---

## Part 1: Why We’re in a Trust Recession

### 1. Information Overload

There are endless experts with conflicting advice. From the viewer’s perspective, it’s impossible to know who’s actually credible.

### 2. Everything Feels Like a Sale

Every post, reel, and DM seems to lead to an offer. Audiences now have their guard up the moment they sense a CTA.

### 3. The Transactional Trap

Most creators focus on **immediate sales** instead of **long-term relationships and brand equity**. This destroys trust before it’s ever built.

**The Goal:**
Move from being a *transactional seller* to an **authentic authority** — someone people go to for advice even when you’re not selling.


---

## Part 2: The Deposit vs. Withdraw Principle (Foundational Concept)

Before you can sell online, you must understand this:

**Trust works like a bank account.**

• Deposits = value, education, stories, insight, perspective
• Withdrawals = offers, CTAs, selling

Most people want to withdraw **before they’ve deposited anything**.

### Real Example:

## Lesson 1: Identify your Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

## Lesson 1: Defining Your Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

### 1. What is an ICP?

Your **Ideal Client Profile (ICP)** is a clear description of the type of person you want to work with. It’s not “everyone.” It’s a defined audience with shared characteristics, needs, and goals.
A well-defined ICP helps you:

- Create targeted content that speaks directly to your audience
- Build trust faster because you understand their struggles
- Design a program they’ll be excited to join


---

### 2. Why Your ICP Matters

When you try to help everyone, your message becomes too general and less effective.
Defining your ICP ensures:

- You attract the right clients
- You can market more efficiently
- You build a brand that stands out in your niche


---

### 3. ICP Attributes

There are **seven different attributes** you can use to define an ICP. You don’t need all seven — choose **two to four** that feel aligned with you.
**Examples of attributes:**

- Age - college students, 30+, 50+
- Gender - men/women
- Ethnicity - Asians, Indians, Carribeans, etc.
- Occupation - desk job, CEO's, traveling consultants, 9-5ers,
- Experience level - beginner, intermediate, contest prep
- Goal specific - lose 50+ lbs, build 10lb of muscle, bench press 2 plates, jump higher
- Key differentiator - PCOS, while still eating fast food, without tracking calories, etc.

Example combinations:

- Helping college students avoid the “Freshman 15”
- Helping fathers over 50 lose 50+ pounds


---

### 4. Things to Consider When Choosing Your ICP

Ask yourself these three questions:

1. **Do you understand them deeply?**
You must know their struggles, lifestyle, mindset, and language. If you’re targeting busy moms, you wouldn’t use bodybuilder slang like “get jacked.”
2. **Will they enjoy and benefit from your program?**
Your methods and style should be a fit for them.
3. **Can they afford your program?**
Avoid audiences with no buying power (e.g., middle schoolers). If targeting lower-income groups, offer mid- or low-ticket options.


---

### 5. How to Pick Your ICP

Here are three common strategies:

1. **Your Past or Current Self**
Help people in the position you used to be in — you’ve already been through the journey and speak their language.
2. **People You Already Work With**
Build on your existing client base and demographic.
3. **Story-Based ICP**
Choose a group connected to a personal experience or story (e.g., helping fathers with diabetes because of a family member’s struggle).


---

### 6. Rule of Thumb

Pick an ICP you are **passionate** about. Passion will keep you grounded and motivated, even when things get tough.


---

- Choose 2–4 attributes for your ICP.
- Clearly identify your key differentiator.
- Confirm you:
    - Understand your ICP deeply
  - Can deliver a program they’ll enjoy
  - Have an offer they can afford

## Lesson 5: How to Script

Scripting is one of the most important parts of creating a video. A strong script can carry the entire piece. Even if you just pair it with b-roll a video can go viral. 

**Key Idea:** Every video you create is a story, whether it’s 10 seconds or 10 minutes. Treat it like storytelling, and only post content you’re proud of. The best content creators are the best storytellers.


---

### **4 Steps to Craft Your Script**

1. **Choose Your Topic & Message**
    - Be clear about what you want to share and why it matters to your audience.
  - Example: “Best supplements for weight loss.”
2. **Pick a Strong Hook**
    - Use the provided *[1,000 Viral Hooks](https://docs.google.com/document/d/17jo7np36FxQK1paINjan7nIxqGj55ja-eQaw2G2LQEI/edit?usp=sharing)* guide for inspiration.
  - Types of hooks include educational, comparison, storytelling, day-in-the-life, and more.
  - Example: “Here’s exactly how to lose 30 pounds in 30 days with just three supplements.”
3. **Deliver the Value**
    - Be concise. Cut fluff, get to the point, and fit your message into 30–60 seconds.
  - Use your own knowledge or AI tools like ChatGPT to help draft ideas, then edit in your own voice.
      - This [ChatGPT Link](https://chatgpt.com/g/g-68384fc3ea308191aa5ae6a6450901a5-content-mastery-ai) is specially curated for content.
  - Use engaging visuals or different video formats to match each point so your content stands out.
4. **End with a Call-to-Action (CTA)**
    - Always encourage the viewer to take the next step.
  - Four types of CTAs:
      - **Follow CTA** – “Follow for more weight loss tips.”
    - **Comment CTA** – “Comment below if you agree.”
    - **Share CTA** – “Share this with a friend who needs to hear it.”
    - **Lead CTA** – “Comment ‘guide’ and I’ll send you my free supplement guide.”

## Lesson 7: How to Batch Content

# Lesson Summary: How to Batch Content

## Why Batching Content Matters

- Posting consistently is one of the biggest challenges for creators.
- Many people fail because they try to record, edit, and post one video each day — a process that quickly becomes overwhelming.
- Batching solves this problem by allowing you to create multiple pieces of content in a single session, then spread them out over time.
- The system balances **quality and quantity**. You don’t need to post six times a day like some creators, but posting at least once daily with solid quality is achievable with batching.


---

## The 6-Step Batching System

### **Step 1: Pick a Format**

Decide which type of video you’ll film in that session. Examples:

- Talking head
- Split screen
- B-roll montage
- Trend/reaction
- Day-in-the-life vlog
- Skits, overlays, voiceovers, interviews, green screens, etc.

### **Step 2: Choose 3–7 Topics in That Format**

- Brainstorm 3–7 video ideas using the same style.
- Example: For split screen videos, you might script ideas like *fit vs. unfit*, *good client vs. bad client*, *best vs. worst foods for fat loss*, etc.
- Keep the format consistent but vary the topics.

### **Step 3: Film All Topics in One Session**

- Record all your ideas in a single filming session.
- In one hour, you can generate 3–7 videos ready for editing.

### **Step 4: Spread Out the Content**

- Don’t post all the videos at once.
- Example: If you filmed 5 split-screen videos, post them over 2–3 weeks (e.g., Monday, Wednesday, Saturday).
- This keeps your content diverse and fresh.

### **Step 5: Repeat with Different Formats**

- Rotate through different video styles to keep your feed dynamic.
- Example: One day you batch split-screens, another day you batch trend videos, another day you batch grocery hauls, etc.
- Over time, you’ll build a large variety of content.

### **Step 6: Batch Edit**

- Dedicate a separate day just for editing.
- Having all videos filmed and then edited together creates efficiency and consistency.


---

## Practical Example (Using Notion for Organization)

- Use a content calendar tool (like Notion) to organize ideas, filming days, and posting schedules.
    - [SAMPLE NOTION CALENDAR](https://www.notion.com/templates/my-calendar?srsltid=AfmBOooxzE6kLMSf4YZRm5Rv_-vpgMgTvbOTP-Gw03KZxmx_m9Dhe96e)
- Plan 2–3 days of filming in a week. Example schedule:
    - **Thursday** → Record 4 split-screen videos (e.g., fat vs. fit, client types, foods, restaurants).
  - **Friday** → Record 3 trending/reaction videos.
  - **Saturday** → Record 4 grocery haul videos (e.g., best fruits, proteins, low-calorie snacks).
- After 3 filming days, you’ll have 10–12 videos — enough for 2+ weeks of daily content.


---

## Takeaways:

- Always aim to stay at least **two weeks ahead of schedule** with batched content.
- Use a mix of **evergreen content** (tips, tutorials, stories) and **timely content** (trends, reactions).
- Keep experimenting with different formats and storytelling to maintain audience engagement.
- Batching is not just about efficiency — it also reduces stress, keeps your posting consistent, and ensures you never miss a day.

## Lesson 9: The 50/50 Content Strategy

# **Summary: The 50-50 Content Strategy**

### **Purpose**

A balanced approach to content creation that helps coaches grow *reach* and *conversions* simultaneously — solving the problem of getting views without sales or sales without enough reach.


---

1. **High Reach, Low Conversion** → Generic content that goes viral but doesn’t sell.
2. **High Conversion, Low Reach** → Overly niche content that doesn’t get seen.

✅ **Solution:** Balance both types of content at a **50-50 ratio**.


---

### **The Simplified Funnel**

- **Awareness Content (Top + Middle of Funnel)** → Brings new people in.
- **Conversion Content (Bottom of Funnel)** → Turns ideal followers into clients.


---

### **The 50-50 Framework**

**50% Awareness Content**

- Broad, relatable, personality-driven content.
- Builds trust and connection.
- Examples: personal stories, humor, recipes, lifestyle, behind-the-scenes, opinions.
- **Goal:** Reach new followers and show who you are.

**50% Conversion Content**

- Targeted, niche-specific content for your ideal client profile (ICP).

- **Goal:** Nurture and convert followers into clients.


---

1. **You are the niche** — your story and lifestyle make your content unique.
2. **Conversion content isn’t meant to go viral** — it’s meant to convert.

    - Awareness → Reach, views, new followers

- Sounding like every other coach
- Only posting business content
- Not posting frequently enough
- Expecting all content to perform the same
- Creating without a plan

### **Key Takeaways**

- 50% Awareness + 50% Conversion = balanced growth
- Post 5–6x per week minimum (ideally 2–3x/day)

## Lesson 10: The Series Blueprint

# **Lesson: How to Start a Series That Nurtures and Converts**

### **Overview**

A content series is one of the most powerful tools you can use to build authority, trust, and emotional connection with your audience.
It’s not just another video format — it’s how you **turn your story into your brand** and your brand into conversions.

This lesson will walk you through:

1. What a series is and why it matters
2. What makes a series convert
3. Common mistakes to avoid
4. Step-by-step process to build your own series
5. The storytelling and scripting framework
6. Editing for emotional impact
7. Final takeaways


---

## **1. What Is a Series (and Why It Matters)**

A **series** is a recurring content format where you share stories, lessons, or insights under one cohesive theme or title.
It’s how you shape your personal brand intentionally, speak directly to your Ideal Client Profile (ICP), and nurture them into loyal fans or clients.

### **Think of It Like This:**

When people think of *Chloe She*, they think of *“30 Lessons by 30.”*
That series became her identity. It told people exactly who she was, what she stood for, and who her content was for.

Your series should do the same — become the **defining element of your brand**.

### **The Goal**

- Speak **directly** to your ICP’s pain points and desires.
- Showcase that you’ve **been where they are** and have overcome the struggles they’re facing now.
- Build **trust**, **authority**, and **emotional connection** that naturally leads to sales — without selling.


---

## **2. Why a Series Converts**

When done right, your series becomes the bridge between your story and your ICP’s current situation.
It positions you as the **guide** who understands their problems and shows them what’s possible.

### **It Works Because It…**

- **Builds trust:** You’re speaking authentically from lived experience.
- **Creates authority:** You show results, not just talk about them.
- **Elicits emotion:** You take words right out of your ICP’s mouth and put them into context.
- **Builds connection:** They start seeing themselves in your story.

When people feel like you “get them,” they’ll naturally want you to lead them.


---

## **3. What NOT To Do in a Series**

Avoid these mistakes that make your series forgettable or feel inauthentic:

### ❌ **Generic Series Names**

Don’t use names like *“Mindset Monday”* or *“Gym Beginner Series.”*
They’re too broad and instantly forgettable.

### ❌ **AI-Generated Scripts**

Don’t use ChatGPT scripts for your series.
They’ll lack **personality, authenticity, and emotional depth** — the exact things that make a series convert.

### ❌ **Empty Backgrounds or Boring Delivery**

Your video needs to be **visually and emotionally engaging**:

- No white walls or flat backdrops
- Use variety in angles, locations, and visuals
- Speak with **energy and tonality** — monotone delivery kills retention

Your storytelling needs to feel real, lived, and intentional.


---

## **4. Step-by-Step: How to Build Your Series**

### **Step 1: Create a Unique Series Name**

Your name should summarize the theme of your story and speak directly to your ICP.

**Examples:**

- *Behind the Grind* — Showcasing the journey of building my online coaching business.
- *Unlearning to Earning* — Speaking to Asian entrepreneurs leaving corporate to pursue entrepreneurship.
- *From Scrubs to Squats* — For healthcare workers transitioning into fitness or entrepreneurship.

Your name becomes part of your **brand identity**, so make it memorable, personal, and relevant.


---

### **Step 2: Brainstorm ICP Struggles**

List out every specific challenge your ICP faces. The more specific you get, the better your episodes will resonate.

**Example — Nurses ICP:**

- Night shifts and rotating schedules
- Constant fatigue and poor sleep
- 12-hour hospital shifts
- Lack of time or energy to work out

**Example — Asian Entrepreneurs ICP:**

- Scarcity mindset
- Pressure from parents
- Fear of leaving stability
- Cultural shame around failure

These struggles become your **episode topics**.


---

### **Step 3: Script Your Episodes**

Each script should take **30–60 minutes** to write.
If it takes you 5 minutes, you’re not digging deep enough.

#### **Tips:**

- **Condense** your words. Every sentence should serve a purpose.
- **Use real-life examples** and personal stories — no fluff.
- **Write like you talk.** Keep it conversational, not robotic.
- **Speak in first person** to invite your audience into your story.

> Example:
> “Growing up Asian, I never ordered soda at restaurants.”
> (Not “If you’re Asian, you probably never ordered soda.” — that feels like you’re talking *at* them, not *with* them.*)



---

## **5. Storytelling Framework for Each Episode**

Here’s a structure you can follow for every episode:

1. **Hook:**
Start with something deeply relatable to your ICP.
    - “Growing up Asian, I never ordered soda at a restaurant.”
2. **Series Intro:**
Introduce your show and the episode theme.
    - “Welcome back to *Unlearning to Earning*, where I share everything I had to unlearn about my culture to earn the life I live today.”
3. **Context / Before Scenario:**
Paint the picture of where you were.
    - “Growing up with this mindset made me scared to invest in myself…”
4. **After Scenario / Transformation:**
Show how you overcame it.
    - “Shifting into an abundance mindset was one of the most freeing unlocks of my life…”
5. **Takeaway / Lesson:**
Deliver a key insight.
    - “It might be worth revisiting your relationship with money — because if you’re not aware of it, you can’t change it.”
6. **Soft CTA:**
Keep it nurturing, not salesy.
    - “If this resonated, follow for the next episode.”
  - “Comment below if you relate.”

These aren’t for selling — they’re for *nurturing*.


---

## **6. Editing for Emotional Impact**

How you edit determines how much your message *hits*.

### **Edit Checklist:**

- ✂️ **Cut dead air:** Remove pauses and filler space to keep pacing tight.
- 🎵 **Use background music:** Match the vibe (inspiring, emotional, calm).
- 🎥 **Add B-roll & text effects:** Use visuals that prove your story — old clips, footage of your journey, screenshots, etc.
- 🎭 **Change scenes/angles:** Every few seconds, keep the visual dynamic.

The B-roll is what **builds credibility** — it shows you *lived* the story you’re telling.


---

## **7. Final Takeaways**

- Your **series is your personal brand**.
- Speak directly to your **ICP’s emotions and pain points**.
- Don’t create a series just to make content — create it to tell your story.
- Focus on **authenticity, depth, and emotional connection** over perfection.
- When you share your story honestly, your audience won’t just watch — they’ll root for you.

> **Remember:** People don’t buy coaching because your program is the best.
> They buy because they trust *you.*
> Your story is the bridge that gets them there.

## Lesson 1: Different Types of Leads

# Lesson Summary: Types of Leads & How to Treat Them

### Why It Matters

Not all leads are equal. Treating every lead the same can either push people away or waste valuable time. Understanding whether someone is **hot, warm, or cold** allows you to respond correctly, nurture effectively, and book more calls.


---

### The 3 Types of Leads

**1. Hot Leads**

**Goal:** Move quickly → book a call ASAP. Don’t let them cool down.

**How to know if a lead is hot:**

- Applied directly through your program link
- Inbound DMs from a hard CTA (e.g., “DM me ‘coach’”)
- Respond to direct promo posts/stories
- Send long, fast, enthusiastic messages

**What do do for hot leads:**

- Urgency is key — people act on emotion. Book a call with them ASAP.
- Mirror their energy (if long messages, try to break up your responses, don’t overwhelm).


---

**2. Warm Leads**

**Goal:** Identify direction → are they closer to hot or cold?

**How to know if a lead is warm:**

- Inbound DMs with curiosity, but not ready to commit
- Responds to **soft CTAs** (polls, freebies, light engagement)
- Send medium-length or mixed messages (fast but short OR slow but long)

**What do do for warm leads:**

- Match their tone and message length.

- Don’t over-invest if they’re giving short replies.
- Ask questions, explore struggles, gently nurture.


---

**3. Cold Leads**

**Goal:** Provide value, build trust → no selling.

**How to know if a lead is cold:**

- New followers who haven’t engaged
- Short, slow responses (disinterested or unready)

**What do do for cold leads:**

- Share free resources, tips, encouragement.
- Over time, they can warm up and eventually become hot.
- Never chase — it pushes them away.


---

### The Formula for Lead Temperature

**Time of Response + Length of Response = Lead Temperature**

- Fast + Long = Hot
- Slow + Long = Warm
- Fast + Short = Warm
- Slow + Short = Cold


---

### Key Rules to Remember

- **Treat leads according to their stage.**
    - Treating a cold lead like a hot one = they’ll run.
  - Treating a hot lead like a cold one = you miss a sale.
- **Mirror their energy and tone.**
- **Never talk more than the lead.** Chasing feels desperate.

    - Don’t just drop a link.

## Lesson 4: Sales Scripts

# Lesson: How to Take a Sales Call from Beginning to End

In this lesson, I’ll walk you through exactly how to take a sales call from start to finish. Sales can feel intimidating especially for us Asians who don’t want to come off as “salesy.” This lesson will teach you a sales script that’s authentic and not pushy. It combines being yourself while unlearning fears around selling a good service.


---

## Why Sales Skills Matter

Sales skills directly impact your success as a coach.
Closing 40% of calls instead of 20% doubles your revenue, even assuming equal effort. So improving your sales approach is one of the most effective ways to grow your business.


---

## 3 Rules for High-Ticket Sales Calls

1. **Get on video:** Use Zoom, FaceTime, or any video platform. Face-to-face interaction builds trust.
2. **Take notes:** Good salespeople always take notes during calls.
3. **Be confident:** Be authentic and confident.

*How to build confidence?* Be prepared.


---

## Pre-Meeting Preparation

Before each call, do these tasks to feel ready and confident:

- Send a video reminder the morning of the call.
Example: “Hi John, just wanted to send a quick video to make sure we’re all set for our call today. Super excited to chat! It should take around 30 minutes. Bring a pen and paper—you’re going to get a ton of value. Talk soon!”
- Review the lead’s info and notes from Calendly or your CRM.
- Record client name, date/time of meeting, phone, email, attendance (Y/N).
- Do anything that loosens you up: jump around, play music, meditate, yell, whatever helps you get confident

## Sales Call Structure (30–40 minutes)

1. **Build connection (1 min)**
2. **Frame the call (1–2 min)**
3. **Client intro / discovery (5 min)**
4. **Follow-up questions (5–10 min)**
    - Clarify goals
  - Why now?
  - What’s worked or not?
  - Create the gap
  - Bridge the gap
5. **Program demo/explanation (5 min)**
6. **Close (5 min)**
7. **Handle objections (5 min)**

Start casual and friendly. Example:
“Where are you calling from?”

Explain the call’s purpose. Example:

## Lesson 6: Overcoming Common Objections

# **Handling Common Sales Objections**

**Introduction**
Sales objections are natural, and handling them confidently is key to closing calls. The goal is not to “sell” in a pushy way but think of it as guiding and HELPING prospects to make decisions that actually benefits them. If you have an excellent service to deliver on the other end of their "yes" then you should not feel bad selling. Period.


---

## **1. Mindset for Selling**

## **Give Yourself Permission to Sell**

- Come from a place of service, not pressure.
- Believe in your program’s results.
- Understand that your delivery is valuable and people need your help.


---

## **2. Handle Objections Before They Arise**

**Best time to address objections:** During the discovery portion of the call. Include questions and statements that handles them beforehand.

**Key strategy:** Watch for red flags and ask follow-up questions early.


---

## **3. Common Objections & How to Handle Them**

### **Objection #1: “I don’t have the time.”**

### **Discovery Phase**

**Questions to Ask:**

- How much time do you currently commit to exercise and nutrition per week?
- How much time can you realistically commit?

**What to Say:**

> “I get it, you’re super busy, I am too haha. But to reach your goal, like losing 20 pounds, you’ll need at least three 45-minute workouts per week and some changes in nutrition. Do you think that’s doable?”


### **End-of-Call Handling**

- If they bring up time again, reference earlier discussion:

> “I remember earlier in this call you said you'd be able to commit to 3 45-minute workouts per week. If I build a program like that, do you still think thats doable for you?"



---

## **Objection #2: “I want to commit later.”**

### **Discovery Phase**

**Questions to Ask:**

- Why now? Why not six months ago or six months from now?

**What to Say:**

> “I just want to understand—what made now the right time to reach out? Like why didn't you reach out 6 months ago? Or even 6 months from now?”
> *Listen for motivation, wake-up moments, or urgency triggers.*


### **End-of-Call Handling**

- **Use their answer as ammunition:**

> “Look bro, you said you’re tired of procrastinating earlier in this call and thats the reason why you're on this call right now. Is starting later really what you want to do?"


- **Offer incentive:**

> “So what I can do for you John is because I dont know my availability a month from now, I can actually get you set up now to secure your spot, start programming for you, so we can secure your spot and when you’re ready to start, we hit the ground running. How does that sound?"


- **Handle with confidence:**

> “I totally understand that you want to start later, but as your coach bro, my first step as your coach, is to help you with this decision on this call."



---

## **Objection #3: “I don’t have the money / it’s outside my budget.”**

### **Discovery Phase**

**Questions to Ask:**

- How often do you eat out or spend eating out? Drinking? Partying? Smoking? (Starbucks, Boba, drinks)?

**What to Say:**

> “Sounds like you spend roughly $X/week on eating out and drinks. Is that right?”


### **End-of-Call Handling**

- **Reference spending habits:**

> “Look bro you're already spending $100 / week on your health... you're just spending it on the wrong things. Eating out...drinks with friends...partying. Investing in this program doesn't have to be additional, it can just be a reallocation.”


- **Use confidence + guarantee:**

> “I want you to imagine you signed up for a 12 month program. And you were exactly where you wanted to be. And you got exactly what you wanted out of it. Would that be worth it? Look I'm so confident in my program, that I'm willing to take all risk from you. If you follow all protocols for exercise and nutrition and check in weekly,  and you see NO results, I will refund you all your money back. How does that sound?”



---

## **Objection #4: “I need to talk to my partner.”**

### **Discovery Phase**

**Questions to Ask:**

- Does your partner know we’re speaking today?
- Are they typically supportive of your health and wellness goals?

### **End-of-Call Handling**

- **Hypothetical framing:**

> “If your partner said yes, would you do it? And if they said no, would you still do it?”


- **Isolate the decision:**

> "It sounds like you already know what they're going to say. At the end of the day, you're going to be the one showing up, working out, eating healthier, like this is completely your decision—you’re the one showing up and putting in the work. What do you want for yourself?”



---

## **Objection #5: “I need to think about it.”**

- This usually isn’t a real objection—often covers one of the four above.

### **End-of-Call Handling**

- **What to do:** Ask gently about their reasoning, but don’t interrogate:

> “Totally understood. Do you know when you’d be able to give me an answer?”


- **Sell the exit first:**

> “Perfect. Can we tentatively set the same time in a couple days?”


- **Then uncover the real objection:**

> “Just so I’m clear for our next call, what exactly do you feel like you need to think about?”


- This often reveals time, money, partner, or commitment objections.


---

## **4. Action Items**

1. **Practice mock calls**: Friends, family, or colleagues. Focus on objection handling.
2. Add in all the pre-objection questions to your discovery call
3. **Track common objections**: Identify patterns and refine your approach.

**Pro Tip:** Practice relentlessly—10, 20, 50 mock objections until you feel natural and confident. Objection handling should feel organic, not scripted.

## Lesson 8: Risk Reversal & Guarantees

# **Risk Reversal & Guarantees – Summary**

### **Why Guarantees Matter**

Guarantees are a huge selling point because:

- People buying online often feel like it’s “too risky.”
- Guarantees lower that fear and give prospects confidence to move forward.
- An offer with **zero risk** appeals to everyone, so take advantage of this


---

### **3 Types of Guarantees**

#### **1. Unconditional Guarantee**

- Example: *“30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked.”*
    - Shows absolute confidence in your program.
  - Pushes people off the fence to buy.
- If you’re worried people will abuse it → make your program so valuable in the first 30 days they don’t even think about asking for a refund.
- The amount of people that this will convince will outnumber the people that actually utilize it, which will lead to more growth.


---

#### **2. Conditional Guarantee**

- Example: *“If you follow all protocol, check in weekly, and finish the program with no results — I’ll refund you in full.”*
    - Removes risk but sets clear expectations.
- Phrasing is key → conditions should be realistic but tough to fail if they actually do the work.
- Protects you from quitters while keeping clients accountable.


---

#### **3. Anti-Guarantee**

- Example: *“We’re so confident in our track record that we don’t need guarantees. We already know it works.”*
    - Signals authority and confidence.
  - Only use this if you’ve got proven results and testimonials to back it up


---

### **How to Use It in a Call**

1. Identify they’re on the fence.
    - *“Got it, sounds like you’re considering it but not fully sure.”*
2. Ask: *“If I got you exactly where you wanted to be, would the investment be worth it?”*
3. Transition into the guarantee:
    - *“Here’s what I’ll do. If you follow the program exactly — workouts, meal plan, weekly check-ins — and you don’t see results by the end, I’ll give you your money back. I’m that confident.”*
4. Close with: *“So either you follow the program and hit your goals, or follow the program and get no results so I'll give you all your money back. Sound fair?”*


---

### **Action Item**

- Add **either a conditional or unconditional guarantee** to your offer and sales script
- Phrase it carefully so the client feels confident, and you stay protected.

## Lesson 9: Creating a Mid Ticket Offer

# **How to Create, Structure & Sell a Mid-Ticket Offer**

### **Why Mid-Ticket Matters**

- If you only sell high-ticket, you’re leaving money on the table.
- Mid-ticket gives you a **downswing option** for people who can’t afford high-ticket.
- Collect more revenue **without undercutting** your main offer.


---

### **How to Structure Your Mid-Ticket Offer**

- Keep it similar to your high-ticket → just **small tweaks**:
    - Weekly check-ins → **bi-weekly check-ins**
  - Loom/video check-ins → **DM check-ins**
  - You coaching → **support coach**
- Price: **60–70%** of your high-ticket program.

⚠️ Rule: **Never lead with mid-ticket.**
Use it only when:

1. Prospect rejects your high-ticket.
2. You’re talking to a lower-qualified lead you wouldn’t normally sell high-ticket to.


---

### **Scenario 1: High-Ticket Rejection → Mid-Ticket**

**3-Step Process:**

1. **Budget Check:**
    - “I’d hate for finances to be the reason we can’t work together. Did you have a budget in mind?”
2. **Make It Special:**
    - “Honestly, I don’t normally do this, but since I know you’d crush it, if I could bring the price closer to your budget, would that change your answer to a yes?”
  - If they still hesitate → uncover other objections first (spouse, time, trust, etc.).
3. **Present the New Offer (Don’t call it mid-ticket):**
    - “Here’s what I can do for you… instead of weekly check-ins, I’ll make it bi-weekly. That way I can bring it closer to your budget at [$X/month], and you’ll still get the same results you came here for. How does that sound?”


---

### **Scenario 2: Selling to Lower-Qualified Leads**

- Make a **price sheet** that compares only:
    - 12-month mid-ticket vs. 12-month high-ticket.
- Clearly show what’s different (bi-weekly vs weekly check-ins, DM vs video, etc.).
- Only show this sheet to people you wouldn’t normally pitch high-ticket to.


---

### **Action Items**

1. Create your mid-ticket program (make 1–2 tweaks, don’t overthink it).
2. Build a mid-ticket price sheet (side-by-side with high-ticket).
3. Update your sales script → high-ticket first, mid-ticket only if rejected or low-qualified.

## Lesson 10: How to Run 14-Day Challenges

# **Lesson: How to Use a 14-Day Challenge to Sign Clients**

### **Overview**

A 14-day challenge is one of the most effective ways for new or growing fitness coaches to **build authority, collect testimonials, and convert followers into clients** — all while providing value upfront.

You’ll learn exactly how to plan, launch, run, and convert from your challenge.


---

### **1. Why Run a 14-Day Challenge**

A 14-day challenge is essentially **free coaching for two weeks** that helps you:

- **Build authority and trust:** If you’re new or have under 20 clients, this positions you as a credible coach quickly.
- **Generate testimonials:** You can’t sign clients without social proof — this gets you both.
- **Show measurable results:** 14 days is long enough for people to see real physical and mental progress.
- **Create momentum:** It gets people excited, engaged, and ready to invest in your coaching afterward.


---

### **2. Step-by-Step Breakdown**

#### **Step 1: Create a Catchy Name + Flyer**

- **Make it attention-grabbing:**
Examples:
    - “14-Day Shredded for Summer Challenge”
  - “14-Day Tone & Tighten Challenge”
  - “14-Day Kickstart Challenge”
- **Design your flyer in Canva. Include:**
    - “Only 20 spots available” (create urgency)
  - “2 weeks of training, nutrition, and accountability”
  - CTA: “DM me ‘FAT LOSS’ for info” or “Comment ‘CHALLENGE’ below”
- **Distribute it:** Post across Instagram, Stories, Facebook, and anywhere your audience is active.


---

#### **Step 2: Outreach & Applications**

- DM **50 engaged followers** — people who’ve liked, commented, or watched your stories.
Use this message:
  > “Hey [Name], I’m running a free 14-Day Shredded for Summer Challenge starting next Monday. You in?”
- If they say yes:
  > “Awesome! It’s 2 weeks of training, nutrition, and accountability — totally free, no catch. Want me to send over the flyer?”
- **Create an application form** (Google Form, Typeform, etc.) and include:
    - Name, email, phone
  - Biggest struggle
  - Fitness goal
  - Why it matters to them
  - ✅ “At the end of the 14-day challenge, I’ll share an opportunity to work with me 1:1 — would you be open to hearing more?”
  - ✅ “Leave your phone number if you’d like a free 15-minute consult to talk about your goals.”

*These questions help you identify warm leads before the challenge even starts.*


---

#### **Step 3: Running the Challenge**

1. **Create a Community:**
    - Use Trainerize or a private Facebook group.
  - Don’t admit participants until the start date — you want everyone to begin together for momentum.
2. **Training + Nutrition:**
    - Keep it simple and scalable — e.g., one men’s 4-day split and one women’s 4-day split.
  - Provide basic nutrition guidelines or meal templates.
3. **Check-ins + Engagement:**
    - Get on calls with anyone who left a phone number pre-launch.
  - Check in with all participants on **Day 7** and **Day 14.**
  - Be highly active in the group — shout out workouts, reply to posts, and motivate inactive members.

## Lesson 4: Programming Basics

# **Lesson 4: Programming Basics**

Programming is the foundation of coaching. The better you understand your client’s background, goals, and preferences, the better you can create a training and nutrition plan that they’ll actually follow. This lesson will walk you through what’s needed to program effectively and the core principles to keep in mind.


---

## **What’s Needed to Program**

### **1. Intake Form**

This is where you collect all the essential information to design a safe, effective program:

- **Basic Info:** Age, height, weight, sex, body fat % (if available).
- **Health History:** Injuries, allergies, surgeries, illnesses, medications.
- **Current Habits:** Nutrition quality, sleep quality, stress, daily energy levels.
- **Goals:** Both physical (fat loss, muscle gain, endurance, aesthetics) and non-physical (confidence, discipline, energy).
- **Fitness Experience:** Previous training styles, current plan, diet history, familiarity with lifting/cardio.
- **Other Considerations:** Biggest roadblocks, mindset/confidence levels, past struggles.


---

### **2. Programming Preferences Form**

Helps align the program with the client’s lifestyle and preferences:

- Days available to train.
- Preferred workout days & duration.
- Training priorities (strength, fat loss, aesthetics, endurance, etc.).
- Exercises they love vs. want to avoid.
- Equipment access (free weights, machines, bands, home vs. gym).


---

### **3. Food Preferences Form**

Nutrition is just as important as training. You’ll want to build a plan around foods your client will actually eat:

- Ask for any food allergies or dietary restrictions.
- Have them **rate foods** within each macronutrient group (proteins, carbs, fats).
- Use their **highest-rated foods** when building their meal plan to ensure adherence.
- Note allergies, intolerances, or cultural restrictions.


---

## **How to Program Training**

Once you have the data, create a plan that matches their goals, preferences, and lifestyle:

- **Assign a Split:** Base this on experience level, goals, and available training days (e.g., full-body, upper/lower, push-pull-legs).
- **Cardio Prescription:** Add cardio in line with their goals (fat loss vs. performance vs. general health).
- **Individualization:** Remember this is your *best initial estimate* — you’ll adjust based on feedback and progress.


---

## **Core Training Principles**

- **Progressive Overload:** The client should gradually increase volume, intensity, or load over time.
- **Adherence & Consistency:** A good program is one the client can stick to. Overcomplicating kills consistency.
- **Keep It Simple:** Complexity doesn’t equal effectiveness. Nail the basics before adding advanced techniques.


---

## **How to Program Nutrition**

Nutrition drives results just as much as training:

1. **Calculate TDEE:** Use their stats and activity level to estimate total daily energy expenditure.
    - [TDEE Calculator](https://www.calculator.net/tdee-calculator.html)
2. **Set Calorie Targets:**
    - Fat Loss → ~10–12x body weight (lbs) as a starting point (or 500-600 calories below maintenance)
  - Maintenance → ~14–16x body weight.
  - Muscle Gain → ~16–18x body weight (or 200-300 calories above maintenance)
3. **Assign Macros:**
    - **Protein:** ~0.8–1.2g per lb of body weight.
  - **Fats:** 20–30% of total calories.
  - **Carbs:** Fill the rest with carbs based on training demands.
4. **Match Food Preferences:** Plug in their top-rated foods into the plan for long-term adherence.
5. **Adjust Over Time:** This is your starting point — check progress weekly and make small tweaks.


---

## **Action Items**

1. Create Intake Forms
2. Create 2–3 “baseline” program templates:
    - Beginner full-body (3 days/week)
  - Upper/lower split (4 days/week)
  - Push/pull/legs/upper/lower (5 days/week)
3. Practice customizing them based on different client scenarios.

## How to Scale - Start Here

# How to Scale to $25–35K Months by Building Your Team

## Why You Need to Scale

When you’re making $10–25K/month, chances are you’re still doing almost everything yourself: client check-ins, messaging, programming, content creation, editing. At this stage, **you are the bottleneck.**

The purpose of building a team is to **buy back your time** and focus on high-income activities (client acquisition, content creation, program development) instead of low-income, repetitive tasks.

Scaling requires you to step into leadership, delegate effectively, and free yourself from being tied to every small task.


---

## The 3 Key Hires for Scaling

### 1. Support Coach (Delivery)

- **Why:** At this point, client fulfillment takes up too much of your time. You’re buried in check-ins, programs, and messages.
- **Role:** Handle back-end delivery—programming, client check-ins, messaging, meal plans.
- **Cost:** ~$1,000–$1,500/month.
- **ROI:** Saves 40–80 hours per month so you can focus on client acquisition and improving your program.
- **Training:** 2–4 weeks to onboard effectively.

> Rule of Thumb: Hire one role at a time to avoid mistakes. Slow, deliberate hiring ensures you find the right fit.



---

### 2. Appointment Setter / VA (Lead Generation)

- **Why:** Once delivery is covered, your next bottleneck is lead generation. If you’re still doing all your DMs, you’re wasting high-value time.
- **Prerequisite:** Only hire this role if you have consistent leads (strong content, follower growth, ads, or at least 100+ new conversations/day). If not, fix your lead flow first.
- **Role:** Open 100–150 new conversations daily, nurture prospects, and book 1–6 calls per day (depending on account size).
- **Cost:** ~$800–$1,300/month (including commission).
- **ROI:** Both time-saving and income-generating, since they handle conversations you would never have time for.
- **Training:** 2–4 weeks to learn your tone, systems, and what success looks like. Initial hands-on guidance is key to keeping them motivated.


---

### 3. Community Manager

- **Why:** As your client base grows, maintaining engagement and culture in your group becomes a heavy lift.
- **Role:** Keep the client community active—posting, engaging, keeping the “fire alive,” ensuring everyone feels connected.
- **Cost:** ~$400–$800/month.
- **ROI:** Saves 3–5 hours/week while improving retention by strengthening client connection and community culture.
- **Training:** 1–2 weeks (lower-skill role, mainly requires energy and consistency).


---

## The Scalable Team Structure

To reach $25–35K months:

- You at the top, focused on leadership and high-value tasks.
- **1 Support Coach** (delivery).
- **1 VA** (lead generation).
- **1 Community Manager** (client retention).

**Total Cost:** ~$2.5K/month.
**Example:** At $25K revenue, you still keep ~$22.5K profit (≈90% margins) while freeing up massive time to grow further.


---

## Key Lessons in Scaling

- Hire one role at a time—train them properly before moving to the next.
- Delegate low-value tasks so you can focus on high-value work.
- Scaling isn’t just about hiring—it’s about **becoming a leader.**
- Set a high standard, lead by example, and create systems that empower your team.


---

## The Bigger Picture

- Getting from $0 → $10K is hard.
- Getting from $10K → $25K is harder if you do it all alone.
- But once you have a team, scaling from $25K → $50K+ becomes much easier because you’re no longer the bottleneck.

Your job now is to **step into leadership, delegate effectively, and focus on growth.**

## The Soft Launch Strategy

# Soft Launch Strategy — Full Lesson Summary

This lesson breaks down **what a soft launch actually is**, **when to use it**, and **how to execute it cleanly without hurting your brand or trust**. A soft launch is not about fear or playing small — it’s a *strategic testing phase* designed to validate demand, refine your offer, and build proof before scaling.

Below is the full framework.


---

## 1️⃣ Why You Soft Launch (The Real Purpose)

A soft launch exists to solve three problems:

### 1. Proof of Concept

Before you spend months building a course or program, you need proof that:

- People actually want the offer
- They’re willing to pay for it
- The problem is real and urgent

Soft launching prevents wasted time, energy, and brand damage.

### 2. First Clients + Testimonials (Without Pressure)

A soft launch lets you:

- Get real coaching reps
- Build confidence in delivery
- Collect testimonials *without* the pressure of a public hard launch

This protects your long-term personal brand and reputation.

### 3. Real-Time Offer Refinement

Instead of guessing:

- What clients need
- What deliverables matter
- What questions come up repeatedly

You refine the offer *while delivering it*.

> If you already have demand, testimonials, and confidence in your offer — you can skip a soft launch and hard launch immediately.



---

## 2️⃣ What a Soft Launch Is (And What It Is NOT)

### ✅ What a Soft Launch IS

- Low-pressure
- Limited visibility
- Invite-only
- Small group or 1:1
- Focused on results, not scale
- Designed to collect proof before going public

### ❌ What a Soft Launch Is NOT

- Avoiding selling
- Being scared to charge
- Low effort
- A “lazy” version of a hard launch

A soft launch is **intentional, controlled, and strategic**.


---

## 3️⃣ When You Should Soft Launch vs Hard Launch

### Soft Launch If:

- This is your **first offer of its kind**
- You’re **pivoting niches or offers**
- You have **no testimonials yet**
- You’re unsure about **pricing, structure, or delivery**
- You value long-term brand equity
- You want proof before scaling

> Example: Pivoting from fitness coaching → business coaching = soft launch first.


### Hard Launch If:

- Demand already exists
- You have testimonials and case studies
- Your offer is validated
- Your systems are dialed
- You’re confident in delivery


---

## 4️⃣ The Soft Launch Framework (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Define Your Beta Offer

Your soft launch offer should be:

- Simple
- Outcome-focused
- Minimal Viable Product (MVP)

Avoid:

- Overbuilding
- Perfectionism
- Complex deliverables

Examples:

- Weekly 1:1 calls
- 8–12 week beta container
- Small done-with-you format

> Your first offer is not your forever offer.



---

### Step 2: Identify Ideal Soft Launch Candidates

You are NOT pitching strangers.

  - Show you’re listening

  - Outcome
  - Limited spots

## The Boosted Post Strategy

## **Lesson Summary: The Boosted Post Strategy**

This lesson teaches you how to use the **Boosted Post Strategy** to rapidly grow your following with people who match your *exact* Ideal Client Profile (ICP). This is specifically for coaches and creators who are struggling to grow organically, struggling to generate leads, or finding it hard to book calls because their audience simply isn’t large enough. If you're already consistently booking 10–20 calls per week or growing 500–1,000 followers weekly from organic alone, you don’t need this strategy.

[Follower Ad Template](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1lpvN4UGd33N7KhJeF8of26VersocwtIQ/view?usp=sharing)

### **What a Boosted Post Actually Is**

A boosted post is a piece of content **designed specifically to bring in followers who fit your ICP** — not to go viral, not to entertain everyone, not for vanity numbers.
Its only job: **attract the right people into your ecosystem.**

### **Prerequisites Before Boosting**

1. **Content MUST target your ICP.**
If your content speaks to everyone, you're literally burning money. People will click, but they won’t follow.
2. **The best boosted post is NOT a viral post.**
Viral posts appeal to broad audiences.
Boosted posts must appeal to a *specific* audience.
3. **Your feed content must already align with your ICP.**
Because when people click into your profile from your boosted post, they should instantly feel like your entire page was made for them.


---

## **Rules for a Boosted Post**

### ✅ **1. Call out your avatar in the first 5 seconds.**

Examples:

- “Busy moms struggling to lose the mom pouch…”
- “Asian guys trying to lose belly fat…”
- “Women working full time who want to drop 10kg…”

This immediately filters the audience so only your ICP engages.

### ✅ **2. Do NOT use direct side-by-side before/after photos.**

Instagram flags these and may reject your ad.

**Workaround:**
Use *cutouts* of the before and after separately on-screen instead of a side-by-side transformation.

### ✅ **3. Caption should be extremely simple.**

Your call to action is ONLY:
**“Follow @____ for [ICP] to achieve [result].”**

This is a follow ad — keep it simple.


---

## **What to Boost**

Start with content that already performs well organically **AND** targets your ICP.
If you don’t have one, create one using this simple script:

### **Boosted Post Script**

“If you are a/an **[ICP]** struggling with **[pain point]**, then follow me here on Instagram where I teach **[ICP]** how to achieve **[desired result]** without **[big objection].**”

Examples shown in the lesson:

If yes → it’s a great candidate to boost.

## ✅ **How to Boost a Reel (Step-By-Step)**

1. **Choose the Reel** you want to boost and click **“Boost Reel.”**
2. **Objective:** Select **“Visit Profile.”**
    - Best for brand awareness and attracting followers.
3. **Audience:**
    - Turn OFF “Advantage Audience.”
  - Choose **Create Your Own Audience.**
4. **Location Targeting:**
    - Add locations where your clients typically come from (U.S., Canada, Australia, Singapore, etc.).
5. **Interest Targeting:**
    - Add interests that signal **wealth, willingness to invest, and entrepreneurial focus.**
  - Examples: Louis Vuitton, Rolex, Tesla, ClickFunnels, Shopify, Apple, entrepreneurship, personal branding, etc.
  - These interests should match what your ICP buys, studies, or follows.
6. **Age & Gender:**
    - Set your ICP’s age range (e.g., 25–40).
  - Select the genders you work with.
7. **Finalize:**
    - Save your audience → Click **Boost Reel** at **$25/day** to start.

## ✅ **How to Analyze a Boosted Post**

1. **Collect 7 days of data** before making decisions.
2. **Check the quality of followers coming in:**
    - Manually look at your new followers list.
  - If most followers match your ICP → targeting is good.
  - If they don’t match your ICP → **fix the targeting**, *not the creative*.
3. **Calculate Cost Per Follower (CPF):**
    - Formula: **Total Ad Spend ÷ Followers Gained**
  - A healthy CPF = **$0.50–$1.00 per follower**
  - Example: Spend $140 → should yield 140–280 followers.
4. **Scaling rules once CPF is good:**
    - Scale to **100 followers/day** for **two weeks.**
  - 100 ICP followers/day should yield:
      - ~1–2 booked calls per day
    - ~10 per week
    - ~4 new clients/week (based on 80% show rate, 50% close rate)
5. **Check Return On Ad Spend (ROAS):**
    - ROAS = **Revenue ÷ Ad Spend**
  - Example: $6K collected ÷ $3K spent = **2X ROAS**
  - Target: **3X+ ROAS**
6. **If ROAS < 3X:**
    - Adjust:
      - Targeting
    - VA conversations
    - Sales process
    - Pre-call VSL
7. **If ROAS > 3X:**
    - Increase ad spend by **20–30% every 2–3 days** inside Facebook Business Manager.

## ✅ **Ad Spend Capacity**

- The **maximum safe limit**:
✅ **Do not exceed 400 followers per day from ads.**
- Why?
    - You (or your VA) cannot start more than ~400 conversations/day.
  - Instagram may flag or block accounts attempting higher outbound DM volume.
- Any ad spend generating followers beyond this point = **wasted money**.

1. **Choose a creative** specifically designed for your ICP.
    - If you don’t have one, create and post it first.
2. **Boost at $25/day** with proper targeting (location + age + interests).
3. **Confirm new followers match your ICP.**
4. **Ensure CPF = $0.50–$1.00.**
5. **Scale to 100 followers/day for 2 weeks** once CPF is good.
6. **Calculate 2-week ROAS.**
    - If **ROAS < 3**, fix bottlenecks.
  - If **ROAS ≥ 3**, scale ad spend +20–30% every few days.
7. **Never exceed 400 followers/day** (max outreach capacity).
